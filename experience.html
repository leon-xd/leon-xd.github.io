May 2021 - May 2022

President

My experience as President of USC Makers was a great taste of management and leadership. Alongside my co-president, Haemin (Jenny) Lee, I managed an Elected Board (E-board) of 12 people through an entire year of the club. The club had grown to a size larger than ever, with over 90 people, nine year-long projects, and four semester-long projects. As president, I managed roles overseeing projects, tutorials, finances, outreach, recruitment, internal logistics, social events, and marketing through weekly meetings.

Highlights:

  • During our tenure as Presidents, we managed the planning and hosting of two external Showcases at the end of each semester, where we showed off progress on our projects in a beginner-friendly manner.
  • We also utilized connections throughout industry in order to obtain Makers' first corporate sponsorships: we recieved donations from Microsoft and Qualcomm.
  • Utilizing our connections throughout USC, we were able to acquire our first independent storage space on campus. This led to the creation of a new E-board role in order to manage the space we were allocated, which was also ideated and executed.
  • Additionally, we coordinated with alumni and industry connections to host four Design Reviews for projects throughout the year.
  • The beginning of each semester drove us to manage a two-step recruitment process involving all of E-board, allowing over 30 more Makers to join the club.
  • We also managed the cultivation of our tight community, where we held over 20 social events to encourage Makers to socialize.

Being at the head of an organization that produced 13 hardware projects with a headcount of over 90 people was an absolutely incredible experience to have as an undergrad. Getting a glimpse at what leadership means in both an engineering context and an interpersonal context helped me grow immensely, and I can now truly say that I love building and growing organizations because of my first-hand experience.

May 2020 - May 2021

Director of Membership

As the Director of Membership of Makers my junior year, I had a few responsibilities to handle. First, I had to lead two recruitment cycles involving all of E-board to let new members into the club. Second, I had to onboard and represent the new members as they got acclimated to the club. Third, I was in charge of DEI efforts for Makers leadership. Fourth, I was in charge of organizing the process of gaining feedback from all members of the club after each semester. Finally, I acted as the point person to resolve interpersonal conflicts within the club's membership and leadership, and was one of the key people in charge of maintaining the Makers community during the virtual semester.

I worked as hard as I could to make sure that my impact on my job went above and beyond what was expected of the role. To start, I completely overhauled the old process of recruitment by focusing on standardizing the recruitment process as much as possible. This came in the form of several improvements through the review of application & interview portions of recruitment. First, I separated E-board reviews into separate spreadsheets so that user bias would not be affected. Second, I wrote a rubric for the application process. Third, I changed the rhythm of interviews to give feedback before discussing with other E-board members. Fourth, I wrote a Python script to attempt to negate grader bias during the application phase. Finally, I specified and delineated what values we were looking for in potential Makers to assist us in making judgements.

In addition to recruitment, I expanded the other areas of my role as well. I started an anonymous feedback form so that members of the club could provide feedback to us without risking their personal identity. Additionally, I started an annual DEI seminar for leadership (all Project Managers and E-board) to ensure that everyone was operating with values that are key to the smooth operation of the club. Finally, I helped to plan, film, and host our club's Spring Showcase, which was streamed live on YouTube to a large virtual audience.

The work that I did this year was extremely gratifying, as holding the community together during the virtual school year was a challenging yet necessary problem to solve. This year taught me how difficult it is to manage and cultivate a community, and it helped me to get introduced to leadership in order to progress through to my next role in Makers.
